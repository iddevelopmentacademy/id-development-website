Custom Erasmus+ Courses in Bulgaria

Flexible training design for schools, NGOs and international groups

This course model is built for institutions that want a tailored Erasmus+ learning experience in Bulgaria. The visual materials suggest a broad offer that can combine ICT, wellbeing, active pedagogy, digital literacy, teaching tools, social media use, remote teaching or school innovation. Instead of forcing one fixed syllabus, this format starts from participant needs and organises the programme around clear learning priorities.

Target groups

Who this course is designed for

School teams

Ideal for teachers, school leaders and support staff joining mobility activities and looking for a programme aligned with their institutional goals.

NGOs and training organisations

Useful for organisations that want a compact international course with practical sessions, peer exchange and adaptation to participant profile.

Mixed Erasmus+ groups

Appropriate when the group includes participants with different roles and a need for a balanced programme that combines shared sessions and targeted workshops.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Define a course pathway that responds to participant needs, institutional priorities and Erasmus+ mobility objectives.
  • Combine practical workshops, reflection and transferable outputs in a way that suits the group profile.
  • Offer a balanced training experience that can include digital topics, wellbeing, active pedagogy or school innovation themes.
  • Support participants in planning realistic follow-up actions for use in their own institutions.
  • Ensure that the programme can be reorganised in 5-day or 7-day formats without losing coherence.

Customisation note

This course can be organised in 5-day or 7-day format and adapted according to the client’s educational sector, participant experience, institutional priorities and preferred balance between theory, practice and field context.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Needs-led design

Before the course, the programme is adjusted to participant profile, sector, experience level and requested thematic focus.

Blended workshop model

Sessions combine expert input, guided practice, collaborative work, reflection and structured feedback.

Contextual learning

The programme can integrate Bulgarian educational context, local examples and cultural components when relevant.

Transfer planning

Participants produce concrete ideas, resources or draft action steps that can be used after the mobility.
