Digital Management Course, Bulgaria

Smarter digital workflows, communication and productivity for education teams

The visual for this course highlights digital management, productivity, collaboration and confident ICT use. Based on that theme, this programme is designed for staff who need to organise digital work more effectively, communicate clearly online and manage files, tasks and shared processes with less friction. The course is practical, workplace-oriented and highly relevant for schools, NGOs and project teams.

ProductivityCollaborationDigital workflowsICT confidence
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

School management and coordinators

Useful for head teachers, Erasmus+ coordinators, department leads and administrative staff who organise shared digital work.

Teachers and trainers

Suitable for educators who want stronger control of digital planning, document management and online collaboration.

NGO and project teams

Relevant for organisations working with documents, schedules, communication streams and distributed tasks.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Strengthen participant ability to use digital tools for organisation, planning and collaborative work.
  • Improve digital productivity through better systems for communication, file management and task coordination.
  • Develop routines that reduce confusion, duplication and lost information in everyday work.
  • Build confidence in selecting the right digital tools for meetings, teamwork, documents and follow-up.
  • Create a realistic personal or institutional digital management improvement plan.

Customisation note

This course can be organised in 5-day or 7-day format and adapted according to the client’s educational sector, participant experience, institutional priorities and preferred balance between theory, practice and field context.

Adaptable programme English delivery Transfer-oriented
Methodology

How the training is delivered

Hands-on digital practice

Participants work directly with common digital tools, test workflows and compare more effective ways of organising information.

Scenario-based workshops

Activities are built around realistic management situations such as planning a project, sharing documents or organising team communication.

Peer exchange

Participants compare digital habits, bottlenecks and good practices from their own institutions.

Transfer tools

Each participant leaves with templates, routines or protocols that can be reused after the course.
