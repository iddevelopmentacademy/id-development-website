Flipped Classroom Training Course

Designing pre-class learning and active in-class practice

The poster emphasises the principles of the flipped classroom model, the use of online resources for self-directed learning and stronger classroom facilitation. This course is designed for educators who want to reorganise learning time: content introduction happens before class, while class time is used for active tasks, discussion, practice and feedback. The programme combines pedagogy, digital tools and lesson design.

Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Teachers across subjects

Suitable for educators who want to redesign lessons and increase active student participation.

Teacher trainers

Useful for trainers who support colleagues with modern instructional strategies and blended learning models.

School innovation teams

Relevant for institutions exploring student-centred learning and more effective classroom time use.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Understand the pedagogical logic, benefits and realistic limitations of the flipped classroom model.
  • Learn how to design pre-class input materials that are short, clear and accessible to learners.
  • Improve in-class facilitation through tasks that use time for discussion, problem-solving and feedback.
  • Develop sample lesson sequences or unit plans based on the flipped approach.
  • Connect flipped learning with digital literacy, formative assessment and inclusive teaching strategies.

Customisation note

This course can be organised in 5-day or 7-day format and adapted according to the client’s educational sector, participant experience, institutional priorities and preferred balance between theory, practice and field context.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Model-and-deconstruct approach

Participants first experience flipped elements as learners, then analyse the design decisions behind them.

Lesson design workshops

The course includes guided planning time to create or redesign real lessons.

Resource testing

Participants work with video, slides, quizzes, prompts and short online materials suitable for pre-class use.

Peer feedback

Draft lesson ideas are refined through structured peer discussion and practical review.
