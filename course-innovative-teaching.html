Innovative Teaching Methods

Exploring fresh approaches to learning that engage, challenge and inspire

This course introduces educators to a range of innovative teaching strategies and methodologies that move beyond traditional instruction. Participants explore project-based learning, design thinking, collaborative inquiry, storytelling and other creative approaches that promote deeper engagement, critical thinking and genuine student ownership of learning. The focus is on adapting these methods to real classroom contexts and diverse learner groups.

Project-based learningDesign thinkingCreativityCollaborative inquiry
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Teachers seeking new methods

For educators who feel their teaching has become routine and want a structured introduction to fresh, evidence-backed approaches.

Curriculum coordinators

For those responsible for curriculum design who want to broaden the school's pedagogical repertoire and promote innovation.

Erasmus+ innovation groups

Ideal for Erasmus+ participant groups whose project focus includes pedagogical innovation, school development or modernisation.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Explore a range of innovative teaching approaches and understand the research behind each.
  • Design and apply project-based learning experiences that promote inquiry and student ownership.
  • Integrate creative and design thinking processes into everyday classroom practice.
  • Use collaborative learning structures that develop teamwork, communication and critical thinking.
  • Select and adapt innovative methods to specific classroom contexts, subjects and age groups.

Customisation note

This course can focus on particular methods such as PBL, design thinking or storytelling, or offer a broad panorama of approaches. It can be tailored to specific subjects, age groups or school development priorities.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Method showcases

Each innovative approach is first experienced by participants as learners, giving them direct insight into what students encounter when the method is used well.

Design challenges

Participants design and prototype their own learning activities using the methods explored, receiving peer and facilitator feedback.

Group project work

Collaborative mini-projects model the kinds of tasks participants can assign to students, making the learning experience deeply practical.

Peer exchange and critique

Participants share their designed activities, critique each other's work constructively and refine their ideas for classroom use.

A 5-day programme combining exposure to methods and practical design work.

Day 1

Day 2

Day 6

Day 7

Expected results and certificates

What participants should leave with

Collaborative project design
