Effective Learning and Teaching in the Classroom

Building evidence-based strategies for active and meaningful learning

This course focuses on how learning works and how teaching can be more effective in any classroom setting. Drawing on current cognitive science and educational research, participants explore strategies for improving lesson structure, student engagement, formative assessment, differentiation and the quality of explanation. The course is practical, reflective and directly applicable across subjects and age groups.

Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Classroom teachers

For teachers at any level who want to strengthen the quality and effectiveness of their everyday teaching practice.

School leaders and mentors

For leaders and mentor teachers who support colleagues in improving lesson design and instructional quality.

Erasmus+ mobility groups

Ideal for international groups seeking a high-impact professional development topic with broad classroom applicability.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Understand key findings from cognitive science and how they apply to classroom teaching.
  • Design lessons with clear objectives, well-sequenced activities and appropriate challenge.
  • Apply formative assessment strategies that inform teaching and improve student progress.
  • Adapt instruction to diverse learners through differentiation and flexible grouping.
  • Develop a reflective professional practice for continued improvement after the course.

Customisation note

This course can be tailored to a specific subject area, school level or institutional priority. The 5-day or 7-day format can be adjusted to balance theory, observation, peer practice and individual reflection.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Research-informed practice

Participants explore what educational research says about learning and apply evidence-based principles directly to their own teaching context.

Practical lesson analysis

Real lesson examples are analysed and redesigned in collaborative working groups, linking theory to practical classroom decisions.

Peer feedback and reflection

Structured peer observation and feedback activities support professional dialogue and develop critical self-reflection skills.

Personal action planning

Each participant creates a concrete plan for implementing key strategies in their own classroom after returning from the course.
