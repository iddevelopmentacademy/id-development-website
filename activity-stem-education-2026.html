About the Training

ID Development Academy hosted an Erasmus+ training programme centred on one of the most urgent priorities in modern education: bringing STEM meaningfully into the classroom. The course — Learning STEM in Education — gave participants direct, practical exposure to the tools, spaces and methods that define excellent STEM teaching today.

Rather than focusing on theory, the programme was designed around doing. Participants worked inside a fully equipped, purpose-built STEM facility — moving between a technology lab with robotics, 3D printing and coding stations, and a dedicated science laboratory — experiencing firsthand how these environments transform learning for students.

Learning by doing: Every session placed participants in the role of the student — working with real equipment, completing real tasks, and reflecting on how each activity translates into powerful learning experiences for young people in their own schools.

The Four Pillars of the Programme

🤖

Robotics & Coding

Participants programmed educational robots, designed autonomous navigation tasks and explored how coding develops logical thinking, teamwork and problem-solving in students of all ages.

🖨️

3D Printing & Design

Hands-on work with 3D printers introduced educators to digital design workflows and the powerful creative and engineering possibilities that additive manufacturing brings to education.

🔬

Science Laboratory

A dedicated science lab equipped with professional tools allowed participants to conduct biology and chemistry experiments — rediscovering inquiry-based learning through the lens of a practitioner.

💡

STEM Pedagogy

Beyond the equipment, the programme explored how to design STEM lessons, integrate cross-curricular thinking and build a school culture where curiosity, experimentation and creativity thrive.

Benefits for Educators & Schools

Investing in STEM professional development has a direct, measurable impact — both on individual teachers and on the schools they return to. Here is what participants gained from this programme:

Confidence with Technology

Hands-on experience removes fear of new tools — teachers leave ready to introduce robotics, 3D printing and coding in their own classrooms.

Higher Student Engagement

STEM activities dramatically increase student motivation. Teachers trained in active STEM methods report stronger participation and curiosity in the classroom.

Future-Ready Teaching Skills

Educators gain the practical skills to prepare students for a world shaped by technology, critical thinking and cross-disciplinary problem-solving.

Erasmus+ Professional Development

Participation counts toward Erasmus+ KA1 accreditation goals, supporting schools' long-term European cooperation and internationalisation strategies.

Ready-to-Use Resources

Participants leave with concrete lesson ideas, activity frameworks and digital resources they can implement immediately upon returning to school.

European Network & Collaboration

Training alongside educators from across Europe builds lasting professional connections and opens doors to future collaborative projects and partnerships.

The STEM Environment at ID Development

The training took place in ID Development Academy's dedicated STEM facility — a modern, purpose-designed space equipped with the full range of tools needed for 21st-century education. The room features an interactive display system, dedicated robotics workstations, AI-enabled learning robots, multiple 3D printers and a fully equipped science laboratory for biology and chemistry experiments.

The design of the space itself is part of the learning experience: open, flexible, visually stimulating and built around collaboration. Participants not only used the equipment — they reflected on how the physical learning environment shapes students' attitudes to exploration, risk-taking and creativity.
