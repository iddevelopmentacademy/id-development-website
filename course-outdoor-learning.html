Outdoor Learning: Adventures in Nature and Urban Spaces

Experiential learning beyond the classroom

The poster combines natural landscapes, urban environments and group-based exploration. That combination points to a course about outdoor learning that uses both nature and public space as meaningful learning environments. The programme supports teachers and trainers who want to develop more active, place-based and wellbeing-oriented methods while also strengthening observation, collaboration and local connection.

Experiential learningNatureUrban spacesWellbeing
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Teachers interested in experiential learning

Useful for educators who want to move learning beyond the classroom and use environment as a resource.

Primary and secondary staff

Suitable for a broad age range because activities can be adapted for younger and older learners.

Project and wellbeing coordinators

Relevant for teams working on inclusion, active participation, environmental awareness or student wellbeing.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Understand how outdoor learning can support engagement, observation, teamwork and wellbeing.
  • Explore practical ways to use both natural and urban spaces for structured educational activities.
  • Design safe, purposeful outdoor tasks linked to curriculum or project goals.
  • Strengthen participant ability to manage logistics, inclusion and reflection in outdoor learning settings.
  • Create adaptable outdoor learning sequences for participant institutions.

Customisation note

This course can be organised in 5-day or 7-day format and adapted according to the client’s educational sector, participant experience, institutional priorities and preferred balance between theory, practice and field context.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Field-based learning

Participants experience tasks outdoors and then reflect on design, facilitation and transfer.

Place-responsive design

Activities are shaped around the educational value of the local environment, not only abstract content.

Reflection and documentation

The course integrates observation, capture of learning and debriefing techniques.

Practical planning

Participants develop activities that are realistic for their own school or organisational context.
