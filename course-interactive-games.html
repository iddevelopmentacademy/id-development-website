Using Interactive Games in the Classroom

Game-based learning for engagement, practice and meaningful participation

This visual clearly centres on interactive games for school staff. The course translates that theme into a practical training experience focused on game-based learning, interactive classroom tasks and the design of playful educational activities. It is especially relevant for teachers who want to increase motivation, participation and retention while keeping learning objectives visible and structured.

EngagementGame-based learningCreative teachingParticipation
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Primary and lower secondary teachers

Particularly valuable for educators working with learners who benefit from movement, play, collaboration and visual support.

School staff looking for engagement strategies

Useful for teachers who want more active methods without losing curriculum focus.

Teacher trainers and non-formal educators

Relevant for facilitators who design interactive sessions for groups of different ages.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Understand the difference between game-based learning, gamification and playful classroom practice.
  • Use interactive games to reinforce learning goals, collaboration and participation.
  • Design low-tech and digital games that are manageable in real classroom settings.
  • Adapt games for different age groups, time limits and group sizes.
  • Create a small collection of ready-to-use activities for participant context.

Customisation note

This course can be organised in 5-day or 7-day format and adapted according to the client’s educational sector, participant experience, institutional priorities and preferred balance between theory, practice and field context.

Adaptable programme English delivery Transfer-oriented
Methodology

How the training is delivered

Experience first

Participants play and analyse educational games before designing their own activities.

Design studio

The course includes guided creation of short games, classroom challenges and reusable templates.

Inclusive adaptation

Activities consider learner diversity, pacing, group dynamics and participation barriers.

Immediate transfer

Every workshop aims to produce game ideas that can be implemented quickly after the course.

Sample 5-day programme

Core course structure

The sample below shows a practical 5-day structure. Content can be refined according to the client’s focus and group profile.

Day 1

Day 1

Foundations of game-based learning: why games work, what they support and how to keep learning visible.

Day 2

Day 2

Low-prep games for classroom energy, recall, language use, teamwork and revision.

Day 3

Day 3

Digital interactive games: quizzes, collaborative challenges, escape-room logic and multimedia tools.

Day 4

Day 4

Design workshop: participants create or adapt their own classroom games around real learning objectives.

Day 5

Day 5

Testing, refinement and implementation planning: peer feedback, adaptation and final showcase.

Day 7
