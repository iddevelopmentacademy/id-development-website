Harnessing Artificial Intelligence (AI) for Education and Beyond

Practical AI literacy, prompting and responsible use for school staff

The poster clearly focuses on artificial intelligence for education and beyond, aimed at school staff. Based on that theme, this course introduces AI as a practical tool for teaching, planning, content support, communication and innovation. At the same time, it addresses the risks and responsibilities that matter in education, including authorship, data protection, bias and the need for human judgement.

AI literacyPromptingResponsible useSchool innovation
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

School staff

For teachers, school leaders, trainers and support staff who need a structured introduction to AI in education.

Innovation teams

Useful for staff exploring digital transformation, lesson design and productivity support.

Erasmus+ participants

Suitable for mobility groups looking for a timely topic with immediate classroom and organisational relevance.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Build foundational AI literacy for educational and professional contexts.
  • Use AI tools more effectively for lesson planning, resource generation and communication tasks.
  • Develop prompting skills that improve quality, relevance and control of AI outputs.
  • Address ethics, bias, authorship, data protection and responsible classroom use.
  • Create a realistic personal or institutional plan for safe AI adoption.

Customisation note

This course can be organised in 5-day or 7-day format and adapted according to the client’s educational sector, participant experience, institutional priorities and preferred balance between theory, practice and field context.

Adaptable programme English delivery Transfer-oriented
Methodology

How the training is delivered

Use-case driven learning

The course is built around real tasks such as preparing differentiated materials, generating examples, summarising content or supporting planning.

Critical experimentation

Participants test AI outputs, compare quality, refine prompts and evaluate limitations rather than accepting results uncritically.

Ethics embedded in practice

Questions of transparency, privacy, academic integrity and human oversight are discussed through realistic scenarios.

Transfer-oriented design

Each participant develops concrete routines or policies that can be implemented after the course.
