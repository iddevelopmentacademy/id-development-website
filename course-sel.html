Social and Emotional Learning (SEL)

Developing social, emotional and interpersonal competencies across the school

Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) refers to the process through which students and educators develop the knowledge, skills and attitudes needed to manage emotions, build healthy relationships, make responsible decisions and contribute positively to their community. This course gives educators a practical framework for embedding SEL into classroom life, school routines and the wider curriculum.

SEL competenciesSelf-managementRelationship skillsResponsible decision-making
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Teachers at all levels

For educators who want to support students' social and emotional development alongside academic learning in the classroom.

School counsellors

For those working directly with student development who want a structured, research-based SEL framework to guide their practice.

Whole-school development teams

For cross-role groups responsible for developing a coherent, school-wide approach to social and emotional learning.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Understand the five core SEL competencies and their relevance for schools and classrooms today.
  • Build personal and professional self-awareness and self-management skills as an educator.
  • Develop social awareness and relationship skills through practice and collaborative activities.
  • Design classroom activities that explicitly teach responsible decision-making and ethical reasoning.
  • Embed SEL into daily routines, lesson structures and the whole-school culture sustainably.

Customisation note

This course can focus on specific SEL competency areas, age groups or implementation contexts. It works well as a standalone course or as a complement to well-being, inclusion or communication-focused training.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Experiential learning

Participants experience SEL activities as learners first, developing first-hand insight into how these approaches feel and function.

SEL competency mapping

Participants map the five SEL competencies onto their current teaching, identifying gaps and opportunities for integration.

Group activities and dialogue

Collaborative exercises build SEL skills directly — participants practise active listening, empathy, conflict resolution and shared decision-making.

Curriculum integration planning

Participants develop concrete plans for embedding SEL into specific lessons, routines and school-wide structures after returning home.
