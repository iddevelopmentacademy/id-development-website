Student Well-being and Mental Health

Supporting student mental health and creating caring school environments

This course helps educators understand the connection between mental health and learning, and develop practical approaches to supporting student well-being in school settings. Participants explore how to identify early signs of stress and emotional difficulty, create supportive classroom conditions, apply prevention strategies and collaborate effectively with families and specialist services.

Mental health literacyEarly interventionSupportive environmentsSchool collaboration
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Classroom teachers

For teachers who want to better understand student mental health challenges and create more supportive learning environments.

Pastoral and welfare staff

For those with a direct role in student support who want evidence-based tools and collaborative approaches.

School management teams

For leaders who want to build a whole-school approach to student well-being that is consistent, proactive and sustainable.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Develop mental health literacy and understand the most common challenges students face today.
  • Recognise early indicators of emotional difficulty and respond in a timely, appropriate and confident way.
  • Create classroom conditions that promote safety, belonging, routine and emotional security.
  • Apply evidence-based well-being strategies appropriate for different age groups and contexts.
  • Build collaborative approaches that connect teachers, families and specialist support services.

Customisation note

This course can be adapted to specific age groups, school types or national welfare frameworks. It works well for cross-role groups combining teachers, pastoral staff and management within one institution.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Case study analysis

Participants explore realistic student well-being scenarios, developing their ability to identify needs, assess risk and choose appropriate responses.

Practical tool exploration

Participants are introduced to a range of evidence-based well-being tools and strategies that can be used in everyday classroom and school practice.

Group reflection

Facilitated group discussions help participants process their professional experiences and develop a shared language for student well-being.

Whole-school approach mapping

Participants analyse their own school contexts and begin mapping the structures, roles and resources needed for systemic well-being support.

Expected results and certificates

What participants should leave with

Mental health awareness

Participants develop a confident, informed understanding of student mental health and the teacher's role in supporting it.

Practical support strategies

Participants leave with a toolkit of accessible, age-appropriate well-being approaches ready to use in the classroom.

Collaborative skills

Participants strengthen their ability to work with parents, colleagues and specialist services for consistent student support.
