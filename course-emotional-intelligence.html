Emotional Intelligence and Emotion Management

Understanding and managing emotions for better teaching, learning and relationships

This course develops educators' understanding of emotional intelligence — the ability to recognise, understand, manage and use emotions constructively in professional and social contexts. Participants explore how emotions affect learning, behaviour and relationships in the classroom and school community, and build practical skills for managing their own emotional responses and supporting students' emotional development.

Self-awarenessEmpathyEmotional regulationPositive relationships
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Teachers and school staff

For educators who want to understand how emotions shape their professional effectiveness and improve their relationships with students.

School counsellors

For those working directly with student emotional needs who want to strengthen their own emotional competencies.

Team leaders and managers

For school leaders and team coordinators seeking to build more emotionally intelligent and collaborative professional environments.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Build self-awareness and understand how personal emotions influence teaching effectiveness and relationships.
  • Develop emotional regulation strategies for high-pressure professional situations.
  • Strengthen empathy and social awareness to improve communication and conflict resolution.
  • Understand how student emotions affect learning and respond constructively in the classroom.
  • Apply emotional intelligence principles to team dynamics and professional collaboration.

Customisation note

This course can be adapted to focus more on teacher well-being, student-facing applications or leadership contexts. The 5-day or 7-day format can be adjusted to match the group's starting point and institutional priorities.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Reflective self-assessment

Participants use structured reflection tools to explore their own emotional patterns, triggers and responses in professional settings.

Role play and scenario work

Realistic classroom and school scenarios help participants practise emotional regulation and empathetic communication in a safe environment.

Group dialogue and sharing

Facilitated group conversations support insight, reduce isolation around professional emotional challenges and build shared language.

Mindfulness and regulation exercises

Participants explore practical mindfulness and self-regulation techniques that can be applied both in the classroom and beyond.
