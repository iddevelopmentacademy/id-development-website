Classroom Management

Creating structured, positive and productive learning environments

Effective classroom management is at the heart of good teaching. This course helps educators build confident, structured approaches to managing student behaviour, creating positive routines, maintaining focus and dealing constructively with challenges. The programme covers both the practical mechanics of classroom organisation and the relational skills that underpin a truly productive learning environment.

Positive behaviourRoutinesEngagementRestorative approaches
Target groups

Who this course is designed for

Classroom teachers

For teachers who want to build more structured, calm and productive classroom environments with greater confidence.

Newly qualified teachers

Particularly valuable for newer teachers establishing their management style, routines and professional authority early in their careers.

School management staff

For leaders who want to establish consistent school-wide behaviour expectations and support staff with management challenges.

Learning objectives

Expected learning focus

  • Understand the principles of positive, relational classroom management and how they support learning.
  • Establish clear routines, expectations and structures from the start of the year or course.
  • Use proactive strategies to prevent disruption and maintain productive learning conditions.
  • Respond effectively to challenging behaviour using de-escalation and restorative approaches.
  • Develop a sustainable, values-based approach to classroom authority and student well-being.

Customisation note

This course can be adapted for specific school levels, age groups or management challenges. It works well for whole-staff groups seeking a shared language and consistent approach to classroom management across the school.

Methodology

How the training is delivered

Scenario simulation

Participants work through realistic classroom management challenges using structured scenarios, developing their repertoire of responses.

Skill practice workshops

Core management skills are introduced, demonstrated and practised in small groups — including voice, positioning and non-verbal cues.

Video analysis

Short video clips of classroom situations are analysed together, building shared insight into effective and ineffective management strategies.

Peer coaching

Participants work in pairs to observe, discuss and give structured feedback on each other's management approaches and language choices.
