About the Course

ID Development Academy welcomed a group of school educators from North Macedonia for an intensive Erasmus+ training week in Bansko, Bulgaria. The course — Practical English Language Course: Foundation for Good Teaching — was designed around a simple but powerful idea: that a confident command of English directly enhances a teacher's ability to access resources, collaborate internationally, and deliver more engaging, globally-informed lessons.

The programme moved beyond traditional language study. Rather than focusing on grammar drills or vocabulary memorisation alone, every session was rooted in the real contexts teachers face — writing lesson plans, participating in international meetings, communicating with European partners, and integrating English-language materials into daily classroom work.

English as a professional tool: The course treated language learning not as an end in itself, but as a practical skill that unlocks better teaching — enabling access to international research, Erasmus+ partnerships, and modern digital resources.

What the Programme Covered

  • Professional English for the classroom — vocabulary, structures and register for educators
  • Spoken fluency workshops — presentations, discussions and peer feedback in English
  • Writing skills for teachers — emails, reports, Erasmus+ documentation and lesson descriptions
  • Using English-language digital tools and platforms in everyday teaching
  • Reading and adapting international teaching materials for local classroom use
  • Cross-cultural communication and working effectively in multilingual European settings
  • Individual language confidence-building through role play and real-world scenarios

"This was exactly what I needed — not just English lessons, but English for my work as a teacher. I left feeling ready to engage with European colleagues and bring international ideas into my school."

— Participant, North Macedonia

Learning Format

Sessions combined trainer-led presentations with hands-on group activities, individual exercises and peer learning. Participants worked with laptops throughout the course, practising real-world tasks: searching English-language teaching databases, drafting Erasmus+ correspondence, exploring digital tools and completing collaborative writing tasks.

The blended approach — combining structured input with immediate practice — meant that participants were able to use and consolidate their English actively from day one, building both accuracy and confidence across the five-day programme.

All participants successfully completed the programme and received an official ID Development Academy certificate of participation, issued under the Erasmus+ framework.

Bansko — An Inspiring Setting

The training took place in Bansko, a UNESCO-recognised historic town at the foot of the Pirin Mountains in southwestern Bulgaria. Its combination of cultural heritage, mountain scenery and a warm, welcoming atmosphere makes it one of ID Development Academy's preferred locations for international programmes. Participants had the opportunity to experience Bulgarian hospitality, explore the old town and enjoy the natural surroundings — making the learning experience truly memorable.
